Glenstal Premier League 2026

The Glenstal Premier League 2026 is a competitive T20 tournament featuring Glenstal Cricket Club and invited teams from across the region. The league delivers fast-paced cricket, strong competition, and an exciting experience for players and supporters.

A high-energy T20 competition showcasing skill, strategy, and exciting cricket as teams battle it out for top honours. Experience thrilling T20 action with fast scoring, competitive matches, and intense moments throughout the tournament.

Season window: Mid April → Mid June

Season window: First week of July → First week of October

Match Format

  • T20 format (20 overs per side)
  • Fast-paced competitive matches
  • Super Over used for tied games

Points System

  • Win: 2 points
  • Tie / No Result: 1 point
  • Loss: 0 points

Fielding Restrictions

  • Overs 1–6 (Powerplay): Max 2 fielders outside the circle
  • Overs 7–20: Max 5 fielders outside the circle
  • Illegal fielding results in a no-ball

Impact Player Rule

Each team is allowed one tactical substitution during the match to influence the game.

  • Must be used before the 14th over
  • Not allowed if match is reduced below 10 overs
  • Can strengthen batting, bowling, or replace an injured player

Toss & Team Sheets

  • Teams submit playing XI and Impact Player list after the toss.
  • Once submitted, cannot be changed.

Over Rate

  • 20 overs must be completed within 90 minutes.
  • Slow over rates will result in fielding restrictions.

Code of Conduct

  • Respect umpires and opponents
  • No abusive language or dissent
  • Anti-doping and fair play compliance
  • Offences handled under Level 1–4 penalties

Player Safety

  • Helmets required for batters at all times
  • Wicketkeepers must wear helmets near the stumps
  • Protective gear encouraged for close-in fielders

Administration & Discipline

  • Captains responsible for compliance
  • Umpires’ decisions are final
  • Disputes handled by GPL Technical Committee

About Glenstal CC

Glenstal Cricket Club, based in Murroe, County Limerick, was founded in 2025. The club promotes cricket within the local community and provides opportunities for players of all skill levels to enjoy the game and compete as part of a growing club.
